Introduction

Today, six out of the seven species of sea turtles are endangered. And most of it is because of human exploitation of natural resources. Thankfully, this hatchery has taken the onus to protect them. They start with keeping an eye on the nesting mother turtles, bringing their eggs to the secure environment of the hatchery and finally releasing the baby turtles into the sea on the day of their birth. Kosgoda is the one of the very few hatcheries where all the five species in the country make their nests. They are the Olive Ridleys, Leatherbacks, Green Turtles, Loggerheads and Hawksbills.

Conservation Efforts

One of the primary activities of the hatchery is locating and protecting sea turtle nests. The staff and volunteers carefully monitor the nesting beaches, identify turtle nests, and relocate them to safer areas within the hatchery. This practice helps to prevent predation, disturbance, and erosion of nests caused by natural elements. Hatchery Facilities: The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery maintains a well-equipped facility where rescued or relocated sea turtle eggs are incubated in a controlled environment. The temperature and humidity conditions are monitored to mimic the natural nest conditions, ensuring a high hatching success rate.

Turtle Species

Sri Lanka is blessed with an abundance of natural beauty, and its coastal waters are home to several remarkable turtle species. These magnificent creatures have been an integral part of Sri Lanka's marine ecosystem for millions of years. The country's tropical climate and diverse coastal habitats provide a favorable environment for turtles to thrive. Sea turtles are a cherished symbol of Sri Lanka's rich biodiversity and play a vital role in maintaining the health of marine ecosystems. Sri Lanka is known for being a significant nesting site for several endangered and critically endangered turtle species. The conservation efforts in the country focus on protecting these turtles throughout their life cycle, from nesting to hatching and their subsequent return to the ocean.

Among the turtle species found in Sri Lanka, the Olive Ridley turtle is the most abundant. These small turtles, with their olive-green shells, can be found nesting along the country's east coast. The Green turtle, known for its vibrant green fat and large size, is another notable species that nests primarily along the southern coast of Sri Lanka. The Loggerhead turtle, distinguished by its robust head, is also found in Sri Lankan waters, although its nesting activities are relatively less common.
